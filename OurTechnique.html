The Craft

Jewelry
Crafting
Process

A step-by-step visual guide on how fine jewelry is created, from initial concept to the final polished piece.

01 of 08
Step 01

2D Design

Conceptualizing the idea. Hand sketching and digital illustrations defining the shape, dimensions, and gemstone placement.

Step 02

3D Design

CAD modeling. Creating a highly precise 3D digital representation to calculate metal weight and view the piece from all angles.

Step 03

Gold Casting

The lost-wax process. Molten gold is poured into a plaster mold created from a 3D printed resin prototype.

Step 04

Jewelry Filling

Refining the raw casting. Removing imperfections, smoothing surfaces, and shaping the metal component using precise files and tools.

Step 05

Stone Cutting

Gemstone faceting. Transforming raw rough minerals into sparkling gemstones by cutting precise facets at mathematical angles.

Step 06

Stone Setting

Securing the gems. Master setters precisely set each gemstone into the metal, using techniques like prong, bezel, or pavé setting.

Step 07

Polish

Final shine. Using high-speed polishing wheels and various abrasive compounds to give the gold a mirror-like finish.

Step 08

Plating

Finishing touch. Rhodium plating for brilliant white gold, or electroplating for yellow/rose gold to enhance durability and color.
